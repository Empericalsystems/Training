Welcome to the IPS/IITA Virtual Training

The International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) in collaboration with the Inter Press Services (IPS) hosts a training program on research evidence dissemination.
The training aims to foster relationships with, and build the capacity of media and youth groups as intermediaries to access, appraise, and disseminate research evidence to the public.
This year, thanks to extraordinary circumstances the world finds itself in because of the COVID-19 pandemic, we have taken our training online. You can find here a list of details you need to know.

    Communication Guidelines

    *House Rules üè°:

    Please read through for some guidance about speaking/chatting on the call

    1. Identify yourself clearly
    2. Include a greeting and state where you are from and what your reporting background is when you first talk to the group
    3. When asking questions be concise, get to the point
    4. Respect others‚Äô privacy by avoiding copying, fliming, recording, quoting or forwarding participant's comments, talks or questions to third parties. IITA resource people can, however, be recorded and interviewed for publication purposes
    5. Avoid excessive use of punctuation and CAPS
    6. Use appropriate language, think, ‚ÄúWould I say this to someone‚Äôs face?‚Äù
    7. Courtesy is key. Be kind. We are all learning together

    *Protocols for constructive criticism ... ü´Ç:

    When doing team evulations please follow these guidelines for caring criticism

    1. Use ‚ÄúI‚Äù language. For instance, ‚ÄúMay I suggest trying methods X, Y, and Z‚Äù rather than, ‚ÄúYou messed up!‚Äù
    2. Sandwich the criticism with positive feedback. ‚ÄúI like your introduction, it is concise and thorough. Let‚Äôs see how we can capture the details in the next section and use that to write up a conclusion.‚Äù
    3. Acknowledge the contribution and offer an alternative, ‚ÄúI see the direction you were going with this and let‚Äôs frame it this way instead, and here‚Äôs why . . .‚Äù
    4. Clarify intentions, assumptions and expectations. For example, ‚ÄúThis appears contrite, was that your intent? Is there another way to say this?
    5. ‚ÄùPut the good of the community of learners and its goals before the critique. ‚ÄúWe‚Äôre all in this together, and we want to meet our goal. Let‚Äôs examine my concern and move on.‚Äù

    *... and how to reactüó£Ô∏è:

    1. Listen to each part of the feedback. Ask clarifying questions. Ask for suggestions. Solidify expectations.
    2. Remain neutral, no need to become defensive.
    3. Take a break. Remove yourself from the situation, team or criticism initially. Approach it again with a fresh attitude and/or more objective stance.
    4. Improve the project based on the feedback, re-submit it.
    5. Respectfully, thank the critic for their observations.
